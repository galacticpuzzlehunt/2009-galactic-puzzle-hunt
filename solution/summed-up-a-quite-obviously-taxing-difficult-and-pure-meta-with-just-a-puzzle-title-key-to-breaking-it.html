Indeed, this meta does not contain any flavortext or shell and we are just left with the answers in some order. Fortunately the title tells us what to do — indeed, the title is key to breaking it! In particular, the title is pangrammatic, meaning that we can create a key containing all of the letters in the alphabet, with the first occurrences of each letter: “sumedpaqitobvlyxngfcrwhjzk”. Next, we should use the “Summed Up” part. Converting each letter to a number in the natural S=0 K=25 order, we may add up these numbers. (For example, consider the answer “THE”. T=9, H=22, and E=3. So their sum is 34.) Of course, some numbers will be larger than 25. The natural thing to do with these numbers is to take mod 26 (for example, 34 mod 26 is 8) in order to ensure that all of our numbers are small enough in order to convert these numbers back to letters, for which we can once again use the same key. (For example, 8 converts back to I.) This gives us, in puzzle order, PICK THE THE MIX, indicating that the item we need from the trash heap to find WALL·E is the cassette tape.

As a side note, this meta answer is extremely thematic because our meta mechanism involves picking letters from a length 26 string, mixing letters in an answer does not affect extraction, and the word the appears three times in the answers. Unfortunately, I wasn’t able to fit three the’s in my answer since we only had time to write 13 puzzles this year.
