Solution: Misplaced Radicals

Obviously, when you first open this puzzle, you are greeted with a series of quotes from Christopher Nolan’s magnum opus The Dark Knight. You’ll quickly notice that a single word in each of these quotes has been changed.

A truly astute solver will look at the groups of words that have been replaced, along with the original words:
  • “...heart/dry...”
  • “...knife/dot...”
  • “...dog/clan...”
  • “...face/slave...”
  • “...old/skin...”
From here, it’s a trivial realization that the only place all of these words appear is in the list of Kangxi radicals (as hinted at in the flavor text of the puzzle), characters used in Chinese writing. Not only does each word correspond to a specific character, but these characters are numbered from 1 to 214. If you “spot the difference” by subtracting one from the other, you get the following numbers:
  • 61 - 51 = 10
  • 18 - 3 = 15
  • 94 - 83 = 11
  • 176 - 171 = 5
  • 125 - 107 = 18
And, of course, when you convert these numbers back into letters (A=1, B=2, C=3...), you get JOKER, who is also the speaker of all the quotes involved. Clever, huh?
